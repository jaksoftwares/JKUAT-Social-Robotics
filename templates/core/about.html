OUR TEAM

We have a team of five MSc. Engineering students at the Department of Computing, JKUAT, supported under the Social Robotics Lab. The team is led by Dr. Eunice Njeri in collaboration with faculty members from the Department of Computing. The lab's projects focus on designing intuitive social interactions by integrating human-centered design, responsible computing, artificial intelligence, machine learning, computer vision, natural language processing, and affective computing. Our goal is to address societal challenges in education, social training, and social care through innovative and impactful research.

EXPLORING THE INTERSECTION OF TECHNOLOGY AND HUMANITY

The lab focuses on creating robots that enhance social interaction and support human well-being through empathetic and inclusive design. Research at the social robotics lab focuses on various aspects of human-robot interaction, including how robots can be designed to understand and respond to human emotions, promote social inclusion, and address the ethical implications of robotic technology. The lab aims to advance the field of social robotics by exploring innovative approaches to designing robots that can positively impact people's lives. This includes studying how robots can be integrated into everyday settings, such as homes and educational environments, to assist with tasks and support individuals with specific needs. The lab also emphasizes the importance of responsible design, ensuring that robotic systems are developed with considerations for inclusivity, ethical design fairness, accessibility, cultural sensitivity, and societal impact.

METHODOLOGY

An innovative learning approach

Our approach to education goes beyond technical proficiency. We start with a strong theoretical foundation in ethical frameworks and human-centered design principles. Students are organized into teams to work on projects that require them to incorporate responsible computing practices into their designs. This hands-on approach is complemented by continuous guidance from faculty and peers, fostering an environment of shared learning and critical reflection. This is followed by project-based learning in a collaborative lab environment, where students apply these principles to real-world scenarios. Teams work on projects that integrate responsible computing practices, guided by continuous faculty support and peer collaboration. The program concludes with a rigorous evaluation process, where students present their projects, showcasing both their technical skills and ethical considerations. This peer-reviewed process serves to reinforce the importance of ethical accountability and collaborative learning.

IMPACT AND SOCIETAL CONTRIBUTIONS

Transformative Outcomes

The JKUAT Social Robotics initiative is already making significant strides. Our projects have led to innovations that support children with special needs, advancements in AI accessibility, and the development of ethical computing applications. These achievements underscore the project's commitment to aligning technological innovation with societal well-being, positioning JKUAT at the forefront of ethical technology education.

REFLECTIONS AND FUTURE DIRECTIONS

Shaping the future of social robotics

As we look to the future, we are committed to addressing the challenges and opportunities at the intersection of ethics and technology. Key lessons from our initiative highlight the importance of data protection and privacy, especially in sensitive sectors like healthcare. Guided by Mozilla's ethical guidelines, we emphasize responsible data stewardship and explicit consent, setting a strong foundation for future generations of technologists.
